# AI-Data-Analysis 📊

## 📌 Aim
Build an AI-powered tool that explains datasets, generates charts, and summarizes insights using prompt engineering.

## 📝 Introduction
This project integrates **financial data analysis, visualization, and prompt engineering**. Using Python, I imported and cleaned a multi-year dataset of call money rates, USD/INR exchange rates, and gold prices. Through preprocessing and normalization, the data was transformed into clear, comparable time-series plots that highlight key economic trends and relationships. Beyond technical analysis, I designed structured prompts to convert raw statistical summaries into professional, step-by-step insights.  

This demonstrates how artificial intelligence can enhance financial interpretation, bridging quantitative results with qualitative explanations.

---

## 📊 Dataset Overview
The dataset contains monthly averages of three key financial indicators from 2019 to 2025:
- **Call Money Rate** – short-term interest rate reflecting liquidity conditions  
- **USD/INR Exchange Rate** – the value of the Indian Rupee against the US Dollar  
- **Gold Price (per 10 grams)** – monthly average market price of gold  

The dataset was sourced, cleaned, and normalized to ensure comparability across variables.

---

## ⚙️ Methods
- Data cleaning and preprocessing (handling anomalies, missing values, formatting issues)  
- Normalization (indexing to 100 for comparability)  
- Visualization (7 time-series and comparison plots)  
- Prompt engineering (role-based, step-by-step AI explanations)  

---

## 📈 Outputs
The following plots were generated:
1. Gold Prices Over Time  
2. USD/INR Exchange Rate Over Time  
3. Average Call Rate Over Time  
4. Normalized Comparison of Gold Price, USD/INR, and Call Rate  
5. Gold Price vs USD/INR  
6. Gold Price vs Call Rate  
7. USD/INR vs Call Rate  

---

## 🧠 Insights
- Gold prices surged during 2020–23, reflecting inflationary pressures and investor demand for safe-haven assets.  
- USD/INR shows steady rupee depreciation, especially post-2020.  
- Call rates declined after 2019, stabilizing in the 3–6% range, reflecting RBI’s liquidity management.  
- Normalized plots highlight gold’s outperformance compared to currency and interest rates.  
- Pairwise comparisons reveal positive correlation between gold and USD/INR, and inverse correlation between gold and call rates.
